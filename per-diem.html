About these rates

The actual contracts. Not HR. Not "market rate." I'm a Bay Area nurse and I got these directly from my nurse friends and union connections. the people who bargained for them. Now you have them too. Because you deserve some transparency, babe 😉
Unfortunately, that's not easy to figure out. Hospitals could make this simple. Of course they didn't. Step numbers don't always equal years. every hospital has their own formula, and no, they won't explain it. This table shows the full range so you can get a feel for whether they're even worth your time.
Each hospital negotiates its own step structure independently. Comparing steps is like comparing apples to oranges. Step 10 could mean 10 years at one hospital and 25 at another. And when you're hired from outside, hospitals use their own formula to determine where you land. Nobody publishes that formula. It's a better comparison to look at years of experience.
Depends on your years of experience. Sutter's base rates start lower but their flat noc/eve differentials are already calculated. UCSF and Kaiser are base rates. add your shift differential on top. Here's how they compare by years of experience:
